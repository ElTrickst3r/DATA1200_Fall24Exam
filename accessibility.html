Manual Measures and Testing for Accessibility

The project is built on a traditional body-style with header, main and footer. We have used semantic HTML with the typical elements such as <strong>, <aside>, <ul>, <table>, <li> and <iframe> in addition to standard heading conventions.

As written in the exam assignment text we have made sure to have a consistent menu- and page-structure with the proper descriptive titles for screen readers.

With regards to images we have ensured that all images have alt-attributes with decriptive text for people with visual impairments.

Our choice of color for the website was originally based on aesthetics and visually passes the eye test. Through automated contrast testing we also see in the test results that the color choice is accessible.

We have of course put effort into making the table on our index page accessible with the proper <th>-element for the header and added a table caption in accordance to most guidelines and automated tests online.

For the pop-out cards on the index site we have also made sure to enable the function to close the cards using Escape-button for users who cannot use a mouse.

Regarding manual testing, we have used Edge, Firefox, Chrome and Safari for PC/Mac, as well as Chrome for iOS and Android. We have also involved four family members and acquaintances as testers. We have made incremental changes along the way, as metioned on the Github-page, readjusting and tweaking as we became increasingly more familiar with the general concept of universal design and accessibility.
